## 1.1 The Fundamentals of Agile Software Development

### Agile Manifesto Values
The foundation of all Agile methodologies lies in the four core values of the Agile Manifesto:
1. **Individuals and interactions** over processes and tools.
2. **Working software** over comprehensive documentation.
3. **Customer collaboration** over contract negotiation.
4. **Responding to change** over following a plan.

*(Note: While there is value in the items on the right, Agile values the items on the left more.)*

### Whole Team Approach
In Agile, quality is everybody's responsibility. The "whole team" (Developers, Testers, Product Owner, Scrum Master) works together to deliver a quality product.
- Breaks down the silos between development and testing.
- Enhances collaboration and communication.
- Testers are involved early in the process (Shift-Left).

### Early and Frequent Feedback
Agile relies on short iterations (sprints) to deliver working software frequently. This allows the team to:
- Get feedback from stakeholders early.
- Adjust requirements based on actual user needs.
- Identify and fix defects quickly, reducing the cost of quality.

## 1.2 Aspects of Agile Approaches

### Extreme Programming (XP)
A software development methodology intended to improve software quality and responsiveness to changing customer requirements.
- **Core Practices:** Pair programming, Test-Driven Development (TDD), Continuous Integration (CI), small releases, simple design.

### Scrum
A lightweight framework that helps people, teams, and organizations generate value through adaptive solutions for complex problems.
- **Roles:** Product Owner, Scrum Master, Developers (includes Testers).
- **Events:** Sprint, Sprint Planning, Daily Scrum, Sprint Review, Sprint Retrospective.
- **Artifacts:** Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, Increment.

### Kanban
A method to manage and improve work across human systems by balancing demands with available capacity.
- **Key Practices:** Visualize the workflow (Kanban board), limit Work in Progress (WIP), manage flow, make policies explicit, implement feedback loops.
